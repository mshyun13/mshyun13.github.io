CSS concepts - Difference between Margin, Border, and Padding

Explanation of Contents, Margin, Border, and Padding

Here are the breif explanation of each different elements.

  • Content - The content of the box, where text and images appear
  • Padding - Clears an area around the content. The padding is transparent
  • Border - A border that goes around the padding and content
  • Margin - Clears an area outside the border. The margin is transparent

Examples of Border

Each HTML element has a border surrounding its content.

This line around this paragraph is the border of this 'div' tag.


Examples of Margin

Every HTML element has four margins that you can toggle: top, right, bottom, and left.

Here are some examples of Margin:

Margin: 20px
Margin-top: 20px
Margin-right: 20px
Margin-bottom: 20px
Margin-left: 20px
Margin: 20px 40px 60px 30px

Examples of Padding

Like with margins, padding has four sides to be declared: top, right, bottom, and left.

Here are some examples of Padding:

Content - Padding: 20px
Content - Padding-top: 20px
Content - Padding-right: 20px
Content - Padding-bottom: 20px
Content - Padding-left: 20px
Content - Padding: 20px 50px 30px 40px
