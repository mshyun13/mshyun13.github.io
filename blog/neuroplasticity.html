Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

14 April 2025

Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

There are 10 principles of neuroplasticity: Use It or Lose It, Use It and Improve It, Specificity, Repetition Matters, Intensity Matters, Time Matters, Salience Matters, Age Matters, Transference or Generalization, Interference. These principles help us understand how the brain adapts to change and learn over time.

By applying these principles, neuroplasticity can significantly improve people's well-being. For example, it supports recovery after injuries by helping individuals adapt to new physical or mental conditions. It allows people to relearn skills that were lost due to injury or illness. Also, neuroplasticity helps in learning new skills by allowing the brain create new neural pathways, strengthen exisiting ones, or replacing that are no longer useful.

Based on the principles of 'Use It and Improve It' and 'Time Mattters', consistently practicing a skill over time helps enhance that skill. The more I train and repeat certain skills, the more efficiently my brain can bulid and reinforce the pathway related to those skills. This helps me to improve my performance and retain what I've learnt.

I believe that exposing myself to a lot of different environments and experiences will improve my neuroplasticity. By facing to unfamiliar or challenging environments, my brain will create new neural pathways. This helps me adapt more easily and learn new skills that are necessary for those environments.

Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

A growth mindset is the belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through effort, practice, and learning. This is opposite of fixed mindset, which assumes that abilities and intelligence are determined at birth and cannot changed.

People with a fixed mindset often see failure as a reflection of their limits and may become discouraged. However, people with a growth mindset see failure as an opportunity to learn and grow. This mindset encourages persistence, and a proactive approach to challenges. It is highly relevant in both personal development and learning.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

During the research about growth mindset, I was surprised to learn that people with fixed mindset often try to exaggeratedly show off their ability and hide their mistake. However, people with growth mindset focus a clear learning goal and don't hesitate to ask questions. They admit what they don't know and try to find out a new approach to solve the problems. This perspective helped me to understand the attitudes that support continuous improvement.

By removing limitations, I will be more open to challenges, more accepting the mistake I make, and focus more on learning rahter than getting right.
