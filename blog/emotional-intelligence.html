Emotional Intelligence

7 April 2025

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence(EQ) is a ability to accurately recognise, understand, and manage personal emotion and emotions of others.

EQ plays a key role in how effectively we navigate social interactions, respond emotionally and behaviorally to the given environment and building meaningful relationships.

EQ can be broken down into two different aspects, Intrapersonal intelligence and Interpersonal Intelligence. Each of aspects includes:

Intrapersonal intelligence:

  • Self-awareness: The ability to recognise and understand persoanl emotion quickly and accurately
  • Self-regulation: The ability to manage and control persoanl emotion in healthy and constructive way.
  • Motivation: The ability to set and pursue goal with a positive attitude and resilience

Interpersonal Intelligence:

  • Empathy: The ability to recognise and understand other person's emotion and their perspective.
  • Social skills: The ability to build and maintain healthy, effective relationships and communicate well within various social environment.

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) focuses the ability of general cognitive abilities, such as reasoning, problem solving, and the ability to learn understanding the new concepts.

It reflects how well someone can processes information, think logically, and apply knowledge.

IQ evaluate the following aspects:

  • Logical reasoning
  • Inference and abstract thinking
  • Numerical reasoning
  • Processing speed
  • Spatial awareness
  • Short-term and long-term memory

EQ (Emotioanal Intelligence) focuses on the ability to recognise, understand, and manage personal and emotions of others.

It plays a key role in personal relationships, teamwork, leadership and managing stress.

EQ evalutate the following aspects:

  • Empathy
  • Motivation
  • Social skills
  • Emotional self-regulation
  • Self awareness

Why is EQ important?

Here are a few reasons why it's important:

  • Stronger relationship: EQ allows us to recognise and respond to the emotions and needs of others. This leads to more meaningful, respectful, and supportive relationship whether with family, friends, or colleagues.
  • Better emotional and stress management: People with high EQ and more capable of keeping calm under pressure and handling negative emotions in healthy ways. This leads to clearer thinking and better decision-making.
  • Improvement on leadership and teamwork: A strong EQ helps leaders inspire and motivate their team, navigate conflicts, and build trust. It also supports collaboration by encouraging empathy, active listening, mutual understanding. This leads to increase productivity and satisfaction.
